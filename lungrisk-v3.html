Experimental screening and nodule research

NTOG Experimental Screening and Nodule Risk Research Hub

Educational browser-only survey and calculators for comparing published screening criteria, person-level lung cancer risk models, nodule malignancy models, growth calculations, and NTOG literature-weighted research scores. Validated model outputs stay separate. The NTOG screening score and post-CT compound score are non-validated research outputs for future Nordic cohort validation.

Start survey See formulas JSON export

Educational boundary

This static page runs locally in the browser. It compares model outputs, flags missing variables, and exports structured JSON for review.

The page does not send, store, or persist entered data. It does not recommend screening, biopsy, surgery, systemic therapy, radiotherapy, discharge, or surveillance. No patient identifiers should be entered.

PLCOm2012 and HUNT estimate person-level future lung cancer risk in ever-smokers. Brock, Mayo, and Herder estimate nodule-level malignancy probability. The experimental ntog_experimental_screening_score is pre-CT and uses no nodule variables. The ntog_not_valitated_risk_score is post-CT and uses person, nodule, growth, smoking, and NTOG modifier domains. Both are unvalidated research indices, not probabilities.

Clinical disclaimer: Use only for education, model comparison, and research planning. Use local validated systems, national guidelines, and multidisciplinary judgement for patient care.

Formulas and score construction

The validated formulas are shown exactly as implemented. NTOG scores are separate, non-validated, literature-weighted research indices. The screening score is pre-CT. The compound score is post-CT.

Important: The NTOG scores are not validated probability models. Their weights are transparent draft weights. They are not coefficients estimated from a Nordic outcome cohort.

PLCOm2012 original

Person-level 6-year lung cancer risk in ever-smokers. Original race/ethnicity and education fields are governance-gated.

lp = 0.0778868*(age-62) - 0.0812744*(education-4) - 0.0274194*(BMI-27) + 0.3553063*COPD + 0.4589971*previous_cancer + 0.5871850*family_lung_cancer + 0.2597431*current_smoker - 1.8226060*((cigarettes_per_day/10)^-1 - 0.4021541613) + 0.0317321*(years_smoked-27) - 0.0308572*(quit_years-10) - 4.532506 + race_coefficient risk = 1/(1+exp(-lp))

HUNT Lung Cancer Model, corrected 6-year equation

Person-level 6-year risk in ever-smokers. Natural logarithms, with +1 before log transformations.

Xb6 = 1.18203062 + 0.31573217*male - 1.98496138*(age/100)^-1 + 1.11994217*ln(pack_years+1) - 0.04002877*cigarettes_per_day - 0.24019955*ln(quit_years+1) - 1.70238304*ln(BMI+1) + 0.08072420*ln(indoor_smoke_hours+1) + 0.49212668*daily_cough risk6 = 1/(1+exp(-Xb6))

Brock / PanCan full model

Nodule-level probability. This prototype reads first-degree relative lung cancer for the family-history term.

lp = -6.7892 + 0.0287*(age-62) + 0.6011*female + 0.2961*first_degree_relative_lung_cancer + 0.2953*emphysema - 5.3854*((diameter_mm/10)^-0.5 - 1.58113883) + type_coefficient + 0.6581*upper_lobe - 0.0824*(nodule_count-4) + 0.7729*spiculation solid = 0, part_solid = 0.377, non_solid_ground_glass = -0.1276 risk = 1/(1+exp(-lp))

Mayo / Swensen pretest probability

Nodule-level pretest probability. Current or former smoker equals 1. Never smoker equals 0.

lp = -6.8272 + 0.0391*age + 0.7917*current_or_former_smoker + 1.3388*extrathoracic_cancer_gt5y + 0.1274*diameter_mm + 0.7838*upper_lobe + 1.0407*spiculation mayo_probability = 1/(1+exp(-lp))

Herder PET-refined probability

Original Herder uses Mayo/Swensen pretest probability. External or Brock-pretest Herder is not implemented.

x = -4.739 + 3.691*mayo_probability + 2.322*faint_uptake + 4.617*moderate_uptake + 4.771*intense_uptake herder_probability = 1/(1+exp(-x))

Volume doubling time

Requires current and prior volume, CT dates, and same measurement method.

VDT_days = ln(2) * interval_days / ln(current_volume_mm3 / prior_volume_mm3)
NTOG experimental screening model v0.3

ntog_experimental_screening_score

This score is a literature-weighted pre-CT screening research index. It is designed to capture risk patterns missed by smoking-only criteria, including never-smokers and newer smokers with strong biological or environmental modifiers. It uses no CT nodule variables, no PET variables, and no VDT.

ntog_experimental_screening_score = min(100, A + T + R + X + H) A = age component, max 15 T = tobacco architecture component, max 25 R = respiratory and fibrosis phenotype component, max 20 X = environmental and occupational exposure component, max 20 H = host, family, prior thoracic RT, and immunosuppression component, max 20 Point bands: A: <40y=0, 40-49y=4, 50-54y=7, 55-59y=9, 60-64y=11, 65-69y=13, >=70y=15 T: pack-years up to 8, duration up to 7, intensity up to 4, current/recent quit up to 4, cap 25 R: IPF 8, non-IPF ILD 5, CPFE 6, emphysema 4, COPD 3, cough/infection-pattern points, cap 20 X: radon up to 5, occupational exposures up to 10, passive/indoor smoke and non-cigarette inhaled exposures, cap 20 H: first-degree/family history, prior thoracic RT, prior cancer, transplant, HIV, steroids, biologics/DMARDs, cap 20 Research signal bands: <20 low, 20-39 intermediate, 40-59 elevated, 60-79 high, >=80 very high. No clinical screening threshold is defined.
NTOG non-validated research index

ntog_not_valitated_risk_score

The score is built as a literature-weighted point index, not as a fitted risk model. Literature supports inclusion of smoking, family history, COPD/emphysema, ILD/pulmonary fibrosis, radon, occupational carcinogens, thoracic radiation, immunosuppression, infection/inflammation confounders, air pollution linkage fields, and nodule morphology. The numeric weights below are draft NTOG v0.3 weights, not validated coefficients.

ntog_not_valitated_risk_score = round(100 * (P + N + G + S + E) / W_available) Weights if available: P = person-model component, max 20 points, max of PLCOm2012 and HUNT percent risk N = nodule-model component, max 35 points, max of Brock, Mayo, and Herder percent risk G = growth component, max 15 points, VDT or simple growth category S = smoking-architecture component, max 10 points, duration, intensity, current smoking, recent quitting E = NTOG emerging-risk component, max 20 points, ILD/CPFE, thoracic RT, immunosuppression, TB/NTM/bronchiectasis, radon, occupational exposures, autoimmune disease, COVID imaging context, diabetes, non-cigarette smoke flags W_available = sum of component weights that are calculable. Missing validated domains are not imputed. Point bands: P: <0.5%=2, 0.5-0.99%=4, 1.0-1.49%=8, 1.5-1.99%=12, 2.0-3.99%=16, >=4%=20 N: <1%=1, 1-4.99%=4, 5-9.99%=10, 10-29.99%=18, 30-64.99%=28, >=65%=35 G: VDT <100d=15, 100-399d=13, 400-599d=10, categorical growing/new=8, stable/decreasing/resolved=0 S: duration up to 5, intensity up to 2, current smoking 2, quit within 5 years 1, cap 10 E: summed literature modifier points, cap 20
