NTOG Expert Network

Nordic expertise in lung cancer diagnostics, treatment, screening, translational research, clinical studies, education, and guideline harmonisation.

Find the right contact View work packages

Overview

NTOG connects clinicians and researchers across the Nordic countries through national lung cancer organisations, work packages, specialty networks, translational groups, and the Steering Committee.

Clinical expertise

Oncology, pulmonology, radiology, pathology, thoracic surgery, radiotherapy, diagnostics, and multidisciplinary care.

Research collaboration

Nordic studies, registry and biobank harmonisation, translational research, screening, prevention, and quality indicators.

Protocol development

Shared Nordic approaches for diagnostic pathways, molecular testing, MDT work, nodule management, and treatment planning.

Education and guidelines

Consensus work, courses, seminars, congress activities, and educational initiatives including NORTHDIP.

How to get involved

Most clinicians and researchers join through their national organisation or through a work package aligned with their expertise.

  1. Contact your national lung cancer organisation

    This is the standard route for clinicians and researchers in Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden.

  2. Join a work package or specialty group

    Work packages organise practical Nordic collaboration in data, screening, clinical studies, guidelines, and education.

    Specialty groups connect experts by field and support protocol development, research, education, and harmonisation of lung cancer care.

  3. Contact the Steering Committee

    The committee includes specialty leads across the five Nordic countries and can guide enquiries to the correct network.

Work Packages

Four streams coordinate Nordic collaboration. Volunteers and contributors are welcome in each.

Full work package descriptions
Work Package Focus Typical expertise
WP1: Information and Harmonisation of Data Nordic registries and biobanks, common variables, frameworks for data collection and analysis. Registry research, epidemiology, biobanking, data science, statistics, clinical quality indicators.
WP2: Screening and Prevention Joint Nordic LDCT screening protocol, prevention strategies, smoking cessation, risk assessment. Pulmonology, radiology, public health, screening logistics, smoking cessation, implementation science.
WP3: Clinical Studies, Medical and Radiotherapy Collaborative trials in early-stage lung cancer, immunotherapy, systemic treatment, and radiotherapy. Medical oncology, radiation oncology, trial design, clinical operations, translational endpoints.
WP4: Guidelines and Educational Activities Consensus on Nordic guidelines, courses, seminars, congresses, and educational activities including NORTHDIP. Clinical guideline work, education, professional training, consensus methods, congress organisation.
