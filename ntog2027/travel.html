13 to 15 May 2027 · Scandic Copenhagen, Denmark

Travel & Accommodation

How to reach the 16th Nordic Lung Cancer Symposium at Scandic Copenhagen — and, if you are not staying at the congress hotel, comfortable options within easy reach of the venue.

Getting here Where to stay
Getting here

Travelling to Copenhagen

By air

Copenhagen Airport (CPH, Kastrup) has frequent direct flights from Stockholm, Oslo, Helsinki and Reykjavík, and across Europe. It is about 8 km from the centre and linked to the city by train and Metro.

By train

Long-distance and regional trains arrive at Copenhagen Central Station (København H). From southern Sweden, the Øresund train from Malmö takes about 35 minutes across the bridge.

By road

From Sweden, the Øresund Bridge connects Malmö to Copenhagen (toll applies). City-centre parking is limited and paid, so public transport is usually easier.

The venue

Scandic Copenhagen

Vester Søgade 6, 1601 Copenhagen V, Denmark

On the city lakes, a few minutes from Vesterport station and within walking distance of the central station, Tivoli and the city-hall square.

Open in Google Maps Open in OpenStreetMap

Getting to the venue

  • From the airport (CPH): take a train or the Metro to København H (about 13–15 min), then one S-train stop to Vesterport — the closest station, about 5 min walk — or walk roughly 15 min along the lakes.
  • From Central Station (København H): about 15 min on foot, or one S-train stop to Vesterport.
  • Tickets: buy in the DOT or DSB app, or tap a Rejsekort; the airport–city trip covers a few zones.
  • On foot or by bike: the centre is compact and very bike-friendly; city bikes (e.g. Donkey Republic) are widely available.
Accommodation

Where to stay

The simplest option is the congress hotel, Scandic Copenhagen, at the venue itself. If you prefer to stay elsewhere, the hotels below are all within an easy walk or one transit stop. Distances are approximate — please book early and confirm availability and rates directly. NTOG is not affiliated with these hotels; they are listed only as a convenience.

Congress hotel

Scandic Copenhagen — at the venue (Vester Søgade 6). The most convenient choice; watch for a room block when registration opens.

Hotels within easy reach

Full-service

  • Imperial Hotel — Vester Farimagsgade · ~5 min walk
  • The Square — Rådhuspladsen · ~12 min
  • Villa Copenhagen — by Central Station · ~15 min

Mid-range

  • Scandic Webers — Vesterbrogade · ~10 min
  • Absalon Hotel — Vesterbro / Central · ~15 min
  • Andersen Boutique Hotel — Vesterbro · ~15 min

Budget & hostels

  • Steel House Copenhagen — by the lakes · ~5 min
  • CABINN Scandinavia — by the lakes · ~10 min
  • Wakeup Copenhagen — near Central · ~15–20 min

Getting around

Copenhagen’s Metro, S-trains and buses share one ticketing system (DOT) — use the DSB or DOT app, or a Rejsekort. The Metro runs around the clock. The city is flat, walkable and exceptionally bike-friendly.

Good to know

  • The symposium runs 13–15 May 2027; book travel and rooms early, as May is busy.
  • Most attendees from the Nordics and the EU/Schengen area need no visa — check your own requirements.
  • Denmark uses the Danish krone (DKK); cards are accepted almost everywhere.

Planning your trip

Registration and any room block open once the programme is finalised — check back or follow #NLCS2027.
