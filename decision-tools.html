Treatment Value Discussion Tool

An educational framework for discussing clinical benefit, toxicity, quality of life, cost, and uncertainty in thoracic oncology evidence.

Open score exercise Back to tools

Purpose

This tool supports structured discussion of treatment value. It is not a formal health-economic assessment and it is not a clinical recommendation engine.

Educational use only

The score is a simplified teaching exercise. It helps make assumptions visible when comparing treatment options. It does not replace trial interpretation, national reimbursement assessment, cost-effectiveness modelling, patient preference, clinical judgment, or MDT decision-making.

Use original trial data, local costs, absolute effects, toxicity data, confidence intervals, subgroup validity, and patient goals before drawing conclusions.

Discussion criteria

The criteria below are simplified anchors for discussion. They should be interpreted with uncertainty, study design, follow-up time, comparator quality, and clinical context.

Criterion What it captures Simple scoring anchor
Overall survival benefit Median or mean survival extension associated with treatment compared with the control arm. Low: <3 months. Moderate: 3 to 6 months. High: >6 months.
Hazard ratio Relative effect on the event rate over time. Must be interpreted with absolute benefit and proportional hazards assumptions. Favourable: <0.70. Moderate: 0.70 to 0.90. Unfavourable: >0.90.
Number needed to treat Number of patients who need treatment for one additional patient to benefit at a defined time point. Favourable: <10. Moderate: 10 to 20. Less favourable: >20.
Number needed to harm Number of patients treated for one additional clinically relevant adverse event at a defined time point. Less favourable: <10. Moderate: 10 to 20. Favourable: >20.
Quality-adjusted life years Combined quantity and quality of survival. Requires explicit utility assumptions. Low: <0.5. Moderate: 0.5 to 1.0. High: >1.0.
Direct treatment cost Drug, procedure, administration, monitoring, toxicity management, and downstream healthcare costs. Low: <10,000 EUR. Moderate: 10,000 to 50,000 EUR. High: >50,000 EUR.
Evidence certainty Confidence in the result, including trial phase, randomisation, maturity, confidence intervals, crossover, missing data, and external validity. Low, moderate, or high certainty. This should modify interpretation, not just the arithmetic score.

Score exercise

Select simplified categories to generate a teaching score. The result is a prompt for discussion, not a decision.

Interpretation

  • 6 to 10: weak or uncertain value signal. Review assumptions and evidence quality.
  • 11 to 15: mixed value signal. Discuss absolute benefit, toxicity, uncertainty, and patient goals.
  • 16 to 21: stronger value signal, provided evidence certainty and applicability are acceptable.

Teaching score

Not calculated

The score is only a discussion aid. Review uncertainty, absolute effects, toxicity severity, patient preference, and local costs.

Limitations

A numeric score can hide more than it reveals unless the assumptions are made explicit.

  • Median OS benefit may not reflect long-term survivors or cure fraction.
  • Hazard ratios do not show absolute benefit and may be misleading if hazards are non-proportional.
  • NNT and NNH depend on time point, baseline risk, endpoint choice, and follow-up duration.
  • QALY estimates depend on utility values and modelling assumptions.
  • Cost thresholds differ between countries and reimbursement systems.
  • Subgroup results may be underpowered or exploratory.
  • Patient goals may override a population-level value assessment.
